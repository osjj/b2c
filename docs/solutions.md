# What PPE Is Required on Construction Sites?

Construction sites expose workers to multiple hazards such as falling objects, sharp materials, heavy equipment, dust, noise, and fall risks.

To reduce injuries and comply with safety regulations, the following personal protective equipment (PPE) is commonly required on most construction sites:

- Safety helmets
- Cut-resistant gloves
- Steel toe safety boots
- High visibility clothing
- Eye protection
- Fall protection equipment when working at height

This guide explains **why each PPE item is necessary**, **when it should be used**, and **how to choose the right equipment** for construction environments.

---

## Common Construction Hazards and Required PPE

| Hazard | Potential Injury | Required PPE |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Falling objects | Head trauma | Safety helmet |
| Sharp steel, concrete, tools | Hand cuts & punctures | Cut-resistant gloves |
| Heavy materials & machinery | Foot crush injuries | Steel toe safety boots |
| Dust, debris, sparks | Eye injuries | Safety goggles |
| Loud machinery | Hearing loss | Earplugs / earmuffs |
| Working at height | Fatal falls | Full body harness & lanyard |
| Vehicle movement | Low visibility accidents | Hi-vis reflective vest |

## PPE for Different Construction Tasks

### Working at Height

Workers on scaffolding, ladders, or steel structures must wear:

- Full body harness
- Shock-absorbing lanyard
- Helmet with chin strap
- Anti-slip safety boots

### Handling Steel, Rebar, and Concrete

Tasks involving rough or sharp materials require:

- Cut-resistant gloves
- Reinforced safety boots
- Eye protection from debris

### Dusty and Demolition Environments

Demolition and concrete cutting create airborne particles:

- Safety goggles
- Respirators or dust masks
- Hi-vis clothing for visibility
